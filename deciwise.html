Project Overview

DeciWise was developed as part of an AI Lab assignment focused on building a simple AI assistant using design thinking. Rather than creating a generic chatbot, I wanted to design a tool that addresses a common human problem: feeling mentally scattered or unsettled when making choices.

The project centers on the idea that many people do not need a bot to decide for them. They need a calm structure that helps them clarify the decision, compare options, recognize trade-offs, and feel more settled in their final choice.

Problem Addressed

People often struggle with decisions because they are balancing cost, time, family impact, uncertainty, values, and emotion all at once. In many cases, the difficulty is not a lack of information but a lack of structure.

DeciWise was designed to reduce that overwhelm by using a question-first approach and guiding the user one step at a time.

Value Proposition

DeciWise does not force a recommendation. It helps the user think more clearly using their own priorities. This makes the tool useful for both small everyday choices and more important life decisions such as job offers, relocation, purchases, or family-related trade-offs.

Design Approach

I used a simple design-thinking approach while developing the bot:

Empathize: identify how people become overwhelmed, second-guess themselves, or feel pressure while deciding.
Define: frame the need as decision clarity rather than decision automation.
Ideate: shape the bot as a calm decision companion rather than an expert authority.
Prototype: build a minimum viable chatbot with a simple interaction flow.
Test: refine the tone, question order, and recommendation style based on actual preview conversations.

Core Interaction Principles

  • Ask one short question at a time
  • Stay calm, clear, and non-judgmental
  • Gather details before offering analysis
  • Use simple comparisons or light scoring when helpful
  • Ask permission before summarizing or ranking options

Supported Wisdom Lenses

  • Neutral
  • Christian wisdom
  • Modern science

These lenses were designed to gently frame reflection, not to dominate the conversation.

What I Refined During Testing

One of the most important refinements came from testing the bot's early responses. Initially, the assistant offered too much interpretation too soon. I adjusted the instructions so that DeciWise stays in question mode first, collects details step by step, and only analyzes once the user is ready.

I also refined the questioning style so prompts remain short but slightly guided, with one or two examples when needed. This made the interaction feel more natural and easier to answer in one sentence or a few words.

Example Use Cases

  • Comparing job offers
  • Choosing between products with different trade-offs
  • Thinking through relocation decisions
  • Making family-related or financially constrained choices
  • Working through uncertain decisions without false certainty

Reflection

DeciWise is important to me because it points toward a wider professional interest: building practical tools that help people think more clearly, not just consume more information. The current version is intentionally simple, but the concept could grow into richer decision-support systems, visual comparison tools, or guided self-reflection experiences in the future.

Why It Matters

Current Scope
