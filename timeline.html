AI and ML Timeline

Project Snapshot

Audience

General professional and academic viewers who need a clear, engaging history of AI/ML.

My Role

Research synthesizer, visual designer, and narrative organizer.

Tools

Canva, AI-assisted planning, and a simple interactive web timeline.

Project Overview

This artifact began as a coursework project on the history of artificial intelligence and machine learning. My goal was not just to list dates, but to communicate the deeper pattern of the field: bold early ideas, periods of disappointment, and modern breakthroughs made possible by better computing power, data, and infrastructure.

The final artifact includes both a polished poster-style image and a lightweight interactive timeline. Together, they show my ability to translate technical and historical content into visual forms that are more accessible and engaging for different audiences.

Poster Version

Why This Format Worked

Chronological clarity: the timeline keeps complex history visually ordered.
Audience appeal: the design aims to feel more engaging than a plain academic summary.
Storytelling: the artifact shows not only growth, but also AI winters and later renewal.
Professional value: it demonstrates research synthesis, design, and communication.

Historical Themes Highlighted

  • Foundational work from Turing and Dartmouth
  • Symbolic AI and expert systems
  • The impact of AI winters
  • Machine learning and AlexNet
  • Deep learning, AlphaGo, and generative AI

What This Demonstrates

  • Ability to summarize technical history clearly
  • Visual design and layout choices for a target audience
  • Synthesis of narrative, academic, and data-driven sources
  • Adaptation of a class assignment into a portfolio artifact

Interactive Version

The interactive timeline extends the poster by allowing a viewer to click milestones and read short explanations. This added interactivity makes the artifact more useful in a digital portfolio, where the audience may want a guided experience rather than only a static image.

Reflection

This artifact was important because it pushed me to think about technical communication as part of professional identity. The challenge was not simply knowing the content, but shaping it in a way that a future employer, instructor, or collaborator could quickly understand and appreciate. It also gave me a useful reminder that design and explanation are essential skills in AI/ML work, not secondary ones.

Audience-Specific Value

A hiring manager or professional reviewer can quickly see that I can take technical material and present it clearly for others, which is important in both engineering and AI-related work.
